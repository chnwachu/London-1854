Design Process

(done in order from top to bottom)

Research

To start I first read the material necessary in "An Introduction to Designing with D3" by Scott Murray to understand what is possible within d3.
Then I looked at the requirements for this assignment to see what I needed to be add. Once done I decided to start with the map since I consider it to be the most important visual tool for the project.

street map

I orginally started using d3.lines to make the streets but realized after sometime paths was a much better option.
Added labels for street after drawing method was complete.

Timeline bar chart

Next I made a Timeline bar chart because it would compliment the map.

deathdisplay

I added a deathdisplay on hover for each bar after creating the graph.

pumps

Once that was made I added in the pumps location to the map so I would have a clear refrence pointplotting to add the deaths.
To make sure they were plotted properly I googled the map of 1854 cholera. I looked back at the files I downloaded to understand the format d3 would best respond to when plotting the dots.

Male and Female death points

Now having a better understanding of the map I started to add male and female death points. Adding age group number reference color code after.
all colors chosen were made with colorblindness in mine. Meaning they should have a high enough contrast that anyone can see the differnce between them.

interactivity

Once the male and female death points were properly displaying I used svg #mapdeaths to introduce interactivity.
Basically stating anytime a bar inside the timeline graph was hoveredover, delete all circles on the map. Then I added in the circle for each death matching the bar death and age range.

Agedisplay

I also added a hoverover display age range feature after so the users could better understand the dots being plotted.

Legend

For good note I also added a legend key to the left so users understand what each dot represents.

Age group bar chart

I added another bar chart at the side displaying deaths by age groups. Added female and male disniction colors and legend afterwords.

Male and Female ratio display

This was added so the users would have an exact understanding of how much of each sex died in the age group.

Scatterplot

Lastly, I added a scatterplot to see if there was any correlation between age groups and the amount of deaths that took place.

Design Decisions

Overall

I wanted my design to be simple and straight forward.
I wanted users to immediately notice the map and bar graph so I made those the biggest. The side charts were made for a more detailed understanding and are not as important. That is why they are on the side.

street map

Made the street and labels black so it would not contrast with the male and female dots being placed on it.

Timeline bar chart

The reason I choose a bar chart for deaths by date was because I thought it would be the easist way to decipher where the data stands relative to the date.

deathdisplay

I added a deathdisplay on hover for each bar after creating the graph. Made so users after exact understanding of how many people died on that day.

pumps

Made them larger then the rest of the circles so that they would easily noticable. Also so users can see if pump proximity increased likelyness of death

Male and Female death points

I choose to use purple and blue for my genders but made sure to look up colorblind contrasting colors so colorblindness would not be an issue.

interactivity

I used hoverover for interactivity between almost all the charts so that users would immedately get the details their looking for without possibly not noticing the details boxfeature. Basically stating anytime

Legend

Findings


Correlation between Age groups and deaths

After viewing the age groups charts, It seems young children (0-10) and the elderly(>80) were most susceptible to the illness.
This is possibly due to weak immune systems young children and the ederly have compared to teen and middle aged people.

Cases seem to spike around the beginning of September


Cases a very slow to start in mid August but as the beginning of September approches.
Espically on Aug 31 where the cases increased by seven times the previous day.
Not sure exactly why this happpened. Possibly could of been large social gathering where multiple people drank from the same contaminated pump.

Illness seem to center around Broad Street


Looking at all the deaths over the past couple weeks, the number of deaths seem to rise the closer you get to Broad Street.
This is possibly due to the pump at the center of the street.
This pump could be the most contaminated of all, making the illness easily transferable compared to the rest of the pumps in the area.
